## RTIPMD Progress Record

### Meaning of "Directed RTIPMD"

Here "directed" does not mean that a complete reaction path is provided in advance, nor that the PES is changed to move toward the product. It only means that a **reaction-coordinate-direction momentum** is added to the initial velocity in RTIPMD.

Implementation in this round:

- Still use `run_rtip_nvt_md`; the real PES is still DeePMD, and RTIP bias is still the local-min repulsive RTIP from the current `deepmd-md`.
- Read `IS.xyz` as the initial structure.
- Read the literature `ts.xyz` or `product.xyz` as the reference target, and reorder the reference structure to match the atom order in `IS.xyz`.
- Do Kabsch alignment for `IS -> target` to obtain an atom-wise displacement direction.
- Normalize this direction into initial velocities according to the requested `initial_temp`.

Therefore the needed prior information has two levels:

- If a TS/product reference structure exists, an atom-wise direction can be used directly; this round uses that approach.
- If no complete TS/product is available and only approximate reaction sites are known, a coarser direction can be constructed, such as moving the CO2 fragment centroid toward the B/N2 reactive core, or assigning local velocities only to CO2/C/O and B/N atoms. This does not require a complete path, but it does require knowing which fragments/atoms should approach.

Note: this only "assigns initial momentum"; it does not constrain the trajectory along the path. If the DeePMD PES strongly repels in that direction, the trajectory may still deviate, rise into a high-energy region, or be pushed away by the RTIP repulsive bias. The purpose of this guided RTIPMD round was to test whether "after giving a favorable initial velocity, DeePMD + RTIPMD can enter the coupling channel."

#### Three RC_K Ranges

| Range | k value | Behavior | Mechanism |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Too weak | 0.001-0.002 | Can approach, but cannot push to bond-forming distances | bias force is insufficient to overcome short-range PES repulsion |
| Critical | 0.004 | Can reach product distances, but PES repulsion > spring restoring force, so it rebounds | just crosses the barrier but k is too weak to hold during rebound |
| **Optimal** | **0.006** | Stable near the target for ~800 steps after arrival | k is enough to resist PES rebound, but not so large as to overshoot |
| Too strong | 0.008-0.010 | inertial overshoot -> N-C bond breaks -> divergence | motion is too fast; reverse harmonic-restraint force causes oscillation damage |

The best-frame bond lengths for k=0.004 and 0.008 are also close to product references (B-O=1.58/1.44, N-C=1.28/1.40), but after steps 293 and 288 respectively the N-C bond breaks: k=0.004 is too weak to hold the system, while k=0.008 has too much inertia and oscillatory damage.

#### Key Conclusions

1. **Step count was indeed the main limitation before.** 200 steps (100 fs) was completely insufficient; 1000 steps (500 fs) allowed k=0.004-0.008 to reach product-level bond lengths.

2. **DPA-3.2-5M + RC-MD k=0.006 successfully validated the `1-tBu + CO2` coupling reaction path.** Starting from the separated state, the trajectory entered the product-basin region under RC restraint and stayed there stably for about 800 steps (~400 fs). B-O and N-C distances (final 1.44, 1.66 A) are within ~0.2 A of product references (1.49, 1.49 A).

3. **`pot_real` is still high.** The k=0.006 best frame has `pot_real = -7.536 Ha` (Erel = +42.8 kcal/mol), much higher than DeePMD's single-point energy for the product reference (-7.642 Ha, -23.6 kcal/mol), a gap of about 66 kcal/mol. This means that while B-O/N-C bond-forming distances are correct, the full trajectory-frame geometry (product-core RMSD = 2.29 A) still differs from the literature product structure - correct distances do not imply correct geometry.

4. **TS target is worse than product target.** The TS B-O reference distance (2.71 A) and N-C reference distance (1.70 A) point in inconsistent directions, so RC restraint struggles to constrain both simultaneously and the trajectory is less stable than product target.

5. **The definition of k=0.006 "success" must be explicit.** Under the previous promising threshold (B-O < 3.0 && N-C < 2.5), all k=0.004-0.010 runs pass. To upgrade to "success", the suggested criterion is: final frame (not best frame) simultaneously satisfies B-O < 2.0 && N-C < 2.0 && pot_bias < 0.01 Ha. By this standard, only k=0.006 passes.

#### Follow-Up Suggestions

1. Run unbiased structural relaxation on the k=0.006 step 589 (best bond) and step 1000 (final) frames to confirm whether `pot_real` can decrease.
2. If `pot_real` remains high after relaxation, inspect specific differences between trajectory frames and the product reference structure, not only the two B-O/N-C distances but also tBu dihedrals and CO2 bending angle.
3. Repeat k=0.006 for other substituent systems (`1-Me`, `1-PMe2`, `1-Ph`, `1-SiMe3`) to validate method transferability.
4. Run a finer scan around k=0.006 (0.005, 0.007) to confirm the optimum.
